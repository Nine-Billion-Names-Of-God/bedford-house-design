## 1. Brief summary of constraints

| Item              | Constraint / implication for planting                                                                                                                                  |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Overall layout    | **Must stay as layered boundary planting with an open central shingle court**. No redesign of the plan.                                                                |
| Fixed elements    | **Do not move** path, gate, front wall/fence, shed, house wall features, tap, existing hedge, neighbour hedge.                                                         |
| Orientation       | **West-facing London front garden**: morning shade, brighter from midday, strong afternoon/evening sun.                                                                |
| Soil              | Typical **London clay**, so plants should tolerate moisture-retentive, fertile soil with occasional summer drying.                                                     |
| Style             | **Structured but natural**; tidy and composed, but not stiff or overly formal.                                                                                         |
| Character         | Should feel appropriate to **Bedford Park / Chiswick Victorian front gardens**.                                                                                        |
| Privacy           | Front boundary planting should give **evergreen partial screening**, but **not read as a clipped hedge**.                                                              |
| Access            | Central shingle area must remain open for **bike manoeuvring**; keep **minimum 1.0 m clearance in front of shed** and maintain turning movement from path into garden. |
| Scale             | Most planting should stay **below 1.5 m**, with taller structure concentrated near the front boundary and rising to **c. 1.8–2.0 m max**.                              |
| House wall bed    | Needs a climber / wall-softening planting, but **tap access must remain clear** and the bay projection must not be visually crowded.                                   |
| Left hedge strip  | Needs underplanting and layered planting that tolerates **root competition and partial shade**.                                                                        |
| Shed relationship | Right side of front boundary bed is compressed by the shed, so planting there should be **shallower, softer, and non-obstructive**.                                    |

### Planting zones understood

| Zone                               | Size / condition | Planting role                                                                                              |
| ---------------------------------- | ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Zone 1 — Left Hedge Strip**      | 1.0 m × 4.6 m    | Improve hedge base; add layered evergreen + shade-tolerant lower planting                                  |
| **Zone 2 — House Wall Bed**        | c. 70–80 cm deep | Soften façade; support one main climber; keep tap usable                                                   |
| **Zone 3 — Front Boundary Bed**    | c. 70–80 cm deep | Create airy evergreen privacy with flowers and seasonal interest                                           |
| **Zone 4 — Central Shingle Court** | Open shingle     | Must remain mostly clear; possible **single compact focal plant** only if it does not obstruct circulation |

## 2. Planting structure for the whole garden

The planting should work as a **three-layer framework** around the retained open shingle centre.

| Layer                          | Purpose                                                                   | Where it goes                                                                       | Typical height |
| ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------- |
| **Upper evergreen framework**  | Year-round structure, privacy, winter presence                            | Mainly **front boundary bed**, with smaller accents in left strip and by wall       | **1.2–2.0 m**  |
| **Mid-layer flowering shrubs** | Seasonal shape, flower, softness, Bedford Park character                  | Front boundary and house wall bed; some in left strip where root competition allows | **0.8–1.4 m**  |
| **Lower perennials / edging**  | Fill soil surface, soften edges, extend flowering season, reduce bareness | All beds, especially hedge base and front bed edge                                  | **0.2–0.6 m**  |

### Overall spatial strategy (planting only)

| Zone                               | Structural intent                                                                                                               |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Zone 1 — Left Hedge Strip**      | A calm, shade-tolerant understorey with a few compact evergreen anchors and repeated ground-layer plants to tidy the hedge base |
| **Zone 2 — House Wall Bed**        | One climber on trellis/wires, with low-to-medium planting beneath; keep a cleaner gap around tap access                         |
| **Zone 3 — Front Boundary Bed**    | Main visual planting zone: layered evergreen shrubs at back, flowering shrubs in front, lower perennials at edge                |
| **Zone 4 — Central Shingle Court** | Mostly open. If used, one compact focal shrub specimen positioned so bike turning space remains clear                           |

***

## 3. Zone-by-zone planting recommendations (general principles)

| Zone                               | General planting approach                                                                           | Avoid                                                                              |
| ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Zone 1 — Left Hedge Strip**      | Tough, repeat-planted, root-competitive species; evergreen winter base; restrained colour palette   | Large thirsty shrubs, floppy perennials, anything needing rich open soil           |
| **Zone 2 — House Wall Bed**        | One elegant climber + low underplanting; plants with some drought tolerance due to wall rain shadow | Dense planting over tap, thorny plants near circulation, overly vigorous climbers  |
| **Zone 3 — Front Boundary Bed**    | Layered shrubs with evergreen backbone and seasonal bloom; privacy should feel soft and airy        | Clipped hedge effect, coarse overgrown shrubs, solid screen planting               |
| **Zone 4 — Central Shingle Court** | Keep mostly empty; optional focal plant only if compact and visually strong                         | Wide-spreading shrubs, thorny specimens, plants that force awkward bike manoeuvres |

## 7. Plant quantities and approximate spacing (general guide)

Because the garden is compact, the planting should be **restrained and repeated**, not overfilled.

| Plant type                          | Typical spacing                 | Rule of thumb for this garden                                                       |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Upper structural shrubs (1.2–2.0 m) | **0.9–1.2 m centres**           | Usually **2–4 shrubs** in the front boundary bed; **1–2** elsewhere                 |
| Medium shrubs (0.8–1.2 m)           | **0.75–0.9 m centres**          | Usually **2–5 shrubs** total per scheme                                             |
| Perennials / clumping lower plants  | **0.35–0.45 m centres**         | Repeated in groups of **3, 5, or 7**                                                |
| Groundcover                         | **0.3–0.4 m centres**           | Use to knit together gaps and suppress weeds                                        |
| Climbers                            | **1 plant per support section** | Usually **1 main climber** (or 1 climber + 1 companion climber if carefully placed) |
