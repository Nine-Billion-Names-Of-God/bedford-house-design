## 1. Summary of constraints

I understand the beds as **two very narrow side beds flanking the patio**, not full-border beds, and the layout is fixed.

| Constraint                                            | What it means for planting                                                                      |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| East-facing garden                                    | Plants should cope with **morning sun and afternoon shade**                                     |
| London clay                                           | Choose plants that tolerate **heavier soil**, or at least cope if planting pockets are improved |
| Very shallow depth: 20–30 cm                          | Use **compact, shallow-rooting, non-woody or lightly woody plants**                             |
| Very narrow width: left 20–30 cm tapered, right 30 cm | Planting must be **single-file or near single-file**, with low spill onto patio                 |
| Right bed near kitchen                                | Should be the **main herb bed**                                                                 |
| Left bed                                              | Can carry **herbs + flowers**                                                                   |
| Water tap on right near house                         | Keep the first section **low and easy to reach**                                                |
| Slatted side fences                                   | Good for **light climbers on wire/trellis support**                                             |
| Low to moderate maintenance                           | Use **simple repetition**, not fussy mixed planting                                             |

## 2. Overall planting strategy

These beds are too narrow for conventional layered borders, so the structure should be:

| Layer                                 | Target height | Function                                           |
| ------------------------------------- | ------------: | -------------------------------------------------- |
| Front / edging layer                  |       5–15 cm | Keeps the bed neat and stops soil showing          |
| Main planting layer                   |      20–35 cm | Main herbs and compact flowering plants            |
| Occasional compact structural accents |      25–40 cm | Small evergreen or foliage anchors, used sparingly |
| Vertical layer on fence               |     1.2–1.8 m | Climbers to soften fence without using bed width   |

### Best approach for these beds

- Use **repeating modules**, not random mixes.
- Keep the **right bed mostly edible and low**.
- Let the **left bed carry a bit more flower and foliage interest**.
- Use **one compact climber per bed** rather than multiple vigorous climbers.
- Keep the **first 40–45 cm of the right bed nearest the tap especially low**.

A good visual structure is:

**front edge thyme / viola ribbon**

- **single row of compact herbs and flowering mounds**
- **one slim climber rising behind**

# 3. Planting Scheme A — herb-forward

**Character:** most practical and kitchen-friendly; green, fresh, simple, lightly flowered.
**Approx. ratio:** about **70% herbs / 30% flowers**.

### Scheme A layout idea

- **Right bed:** mainly parsley, chives, dwarf sage, thyme edging.
- **Left bed:** compact oregano, chives, parsley, campanula, one heuchera accent.
- **Climbers:** compact white/pale-blue patio clematis.

| Item                       | Recommendation                                     |

# 8. Final recommended planting scheme

## Recommended: **Scheme B — balanced herb + flowers**

This is the strongest option for your brief because it does the best job of balancing all the constraints:

| Why Scheme B is the best fit      | Reason                                                                     |
| --------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Keeps the right bed useful        | Still clearly herb-led and easy to harvest                                 |
| Makes the left bed feel softer    | Flowers and foliage contrast improve the patio edge                        |
| Handles the narrow width well     | Uses compact mounds rather than sprawling plants                           |
| Works with east-facing conditions | Herbs chosen are adaptable; flowers prefer morning sun / cooler afternoons |
| Feels designed, not random        | Repetition is strong and easy to read                                      |
| Low-moderate maintenance          | Mostly clipping, deadheading, and occasional parsley replacement           |

### My final practical suggestion

Use this basic rhythm:

- **Right bed:** thyme edge + parsley/chives/garlic chives + one heuchera accent + one compact clematis
- **Left bed:** thyme edge + oregano/marjoram/chives + campanula + one heuchera + spring primroses + one compact clematis

That gives you a bed that is still genuinely usable, but looks intentionally planted rather than like a herb strip.
