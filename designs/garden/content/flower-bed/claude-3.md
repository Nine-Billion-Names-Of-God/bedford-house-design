# Planting Design — Narrow Flower Beds

## London Victorian Terraced House · East-Facing Patio Garden

---

## 1. Summary of Constraints

| Constraint | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Right bed** | 0.3 m wide × 2.1 m long |
| **Left bed** | 0.2–0.3 m wide (tapered) × 2.1 m long |
| **Soil depth** | 0.2–0.3 m (very shallow) |
| **Soil type** | London clay |
| **Orientation** | East-facing — morning sun, afternoon shade |
| **Boundaries** | Wooden slatted fence both sides |
| **Bed offset** | Starts 0.5 m from house wall; ends 0.5 m before patio edge |
| **Water tap** | Right side near house — must remain accessible |
| **Right bed function** | Primary herb zone (near kitchen) |
| **Left bed function** | Secondary — herbs + flowers |
| **Planting ratio** | 60–70 % herbs · 30–40 % flowering/ornamental |
| **Climbers** | Mandatory vertical layer on both fences |
| **Style** | Repetition-based patterns, not random mixes |
| **Avoid** | Large shrubs, deep-rooted plants, patio obstruction |

### Critical Physical Reality

These beds are **extremely narrow** (20–30 cm). There is room for a **single row** of plants only — no front-to-back layering is possible except for very low creeping edging plants (≤5 cm height) tucked at the front margin. Every plant selection must account for this.

At 20–25 cm spacing along a 2.1 m bed, there are approximately **8–10 planting positions** per bed (including one climber at the base of the fence).

---

## 2. Overall Planting Strategy

### Structure

Each bed is treated as a **single-file linear sequence** of plants against the fence, read from the house end to the patio end:

```
HOUSE WALL
   ┊  0.5 m clear gap (tap access on right side)
   ┊
   ├── Position 1  (house end — keep LOW on right bed for tap sightline)
   ├── Position 2
   ├── Position 3
   ├── Position 4
   ├── Position 5
   ├── Position 6
   ├── Position 7
   ├── Position 8  (climber at base of fence — far end)
   ┊
   ┊  0.5 m clear gap
PATIO EDGE
```

### Layering Approach (Vertical, Not Horizontal)

Because the beds are too narrow for depth layering, vertical structure comes from:

1. **Ground plane** — creeping thyme / chamomile trailing along front edge (2–5 cm)
2. **Low herbs** — chives, parsley, compact thyme (15–25 cm)
3. **Medium herbs** — sage, rosemary, oregano (30–45 cm)
4. **Climber** — trained up fence behind the planting line (1.5–2.5 m)

### Plant Selection Principles

- **East-facing tolerance**: all plants must handle morning sun and afternoon shade. Full-sun-only plants (e.g., Mediterranean lavender) are used sparingly and positioned at the sunniest end.
- **Shallow rooting**: maximum root depth 20–30 cm. Deep tap-rooted plants excluded.
- **Clay tolerance**: selected plants cope with moisture-retentive clay or benefit from a 5 cm gravel/grit mulch layer to improve surface drainage.
- **Compact habit**: maximum spread 20–25 cm for main plants; nothing wider than the bed.
- **UK availability**: all plants readily available at UK garden centres (RHS Plant Finder listed).

### Soil Preparation (Common to All Schemes)

- Dig in coarse horticultural grit at 30 % by volume to improve drainage in clay
- Add a thin layer (2–3 cm) of multipurpose compost
- Top-dress with fine gravel or slate chippings (2 cm) to suppress weeds and retain moisture
- For climbers: excavate a pocket 30 cm × 30 cm × 30 cm at planting position and backfill with 50/50 compost + grit mix

### Climber Options Across All Schemes

| Climber | Used In | H × W | Flowers | Evergreen? | Support | Soil Depth Notes |

| *Clematis texensis* 'Princess Kate' | Scheme C (right) | 2–2.5 m × 0.5 m | White tulips, Jul–Oct | Herbaceous (dies back) | Wires or small trellis | Very compact. Hard prune Feb (Group 3). Works in 25 cm depth. |
| *Lonicera ×heckrottii* 'Gold Flame' | Scheme B (left) | 2–3 m × 1 m | Pink-gold, scented, Jun–Sep | Semi-evergreen | Trellis or wires | Slightly deeper roots — ensure full 30 cm depth. Feed annually. |
| *Trachelospermum jasminoides* | Scheme C (left) | 2–3 m × 1 m | White, scented, Jun–Aug | Evergreen | Trellis or wires | Benefits from sheltered position. Slow to establish, then low-maintenance. Needs 30 cm depth. |

### Support Requirements

All climbers require support fixed to the fence:

- **Option 1 — Trellis panel:** Fix a 0.6 m wide × 1.8 m tall trellis panel to the fence at each climber position. Use 3 cm stand-off battens so the climber can twine behind the trellis.
- **Option 2 — Horizontal wires:** Fix galvanised wire at 30 cm intervals using vine eyes screwed into the fence. Cheaper and less visible.
- **Recommendation:** Wires are better for these narrow beds — trellis panels would project too far into the bed.

### Climber Planting in Shallow Soil

- Excavate a pocket slightly wider and deeper than the surrounding bed (30 × 30 × 30 cm)
- Line the base with 3 cm of gravel for drainage
- Backfill with 50/50 multipurpose compost and horticultural grit
- Plant the climber 5–8 cm away from the fence to allow air circulation behind
- Mulch the root zone with slate chippings (3 cm)
- Water regularly in the first year — clay soil may pool, so drainage is critical

## 9. Final Recommendation

### Recommended: Scheme A (right bed) + Scheme C (left bed) — Hybrid Approach

For the best balance of practicality and beauty in this particular garden:

**Right bed — use Scheme A's planting:**
- Maximum herb utility near the kitchen
- Simple sage–thyme–chive repetition
- Easy to maintain and harvest
- *Clematis alpina* provides reliable spring flowers with zero pruning

**Left bed — use Scheme C's planting:**
- Heuchera and dwarf lavender provide year-round structure and foliage contrast
- Hardy geranium 'Rozanne' delivers colour from June to October
- *Trachelospermum jasminoides* (star jasmine) gives evergreen screening and summer scent
- Mexican fleabane at the edge softens the patio line

This hybrid gives you:
- A **fully functional herb garden** on the right (easy kitchen access)
- A **visually rich ornamental border** on the left (with chives for casual picking)
- **Climbers on both fences** for vertical softening
- **Year-round interest** from evergreen foliage plants
- A clear **repetitive rhythm** in both beds

### Maintenance Calendar

| Month | Task |
|---|---|
| **March** | Cut back dead clematis growth (Group 3 only). Divide chives if congested. Replace parsley biennials. |
| **April** | Feed all plants with general-purpose fertiliser. Check climber ties. |
| **May–Sep** | Harvest herbs regularly (promotes bushy growth). Deadhead flowers. Water climbers in dry spells. |
| **July** | Cut back lemon balm and oregano by half to encourage fresh growth. |
| **August** | Trim lavender after flowering — do not cut into old wood. |
| **October** | Clear fallen leaves from beds. Last herb harvest before dormancy. |
| **November** | Mulch beds with 2 cm compost. Check fence supports and trellis/wires. |
| **Year-round** | Keep front edge trimmed so plants don't obstruct patio. Remove any self-sown seedlings in paving joints. |
